№ 01

Fraunces

The current font — baseline reference. Romantic, playful, dramatic italic via the SOFT axis. Cyrillic glyphs are present but flat: no SOFT axis support and the italic Cyrillic feels visibly different from the Latin. The two language versions of the site stop looking like the same publication.

№ 02

EB Garamond

The literary pick. Classical Garamond revival by Georg Duffner. Italic has real personality — swashes, calligraphic ductus. Cyrillic is drawn properly, including Serbian-specific letters. Feels like an 18th-century book in the best way. Trade-off: less modern punch than Fraunces, more bookish than editorial.

№ 03

Spectral

The contemporary pick. Production Type, drawn for screens, intended to work across Latin and Cyrillic from day one. Sharp transitional serif with editorial-newspaper energy. Italic has measurable character without going florid. Most "engineered for what we're doing" of the bunch.

№ 04

Cormorant Garamond

The high-fashion pick. Christian Thalmann's extreme-contrast Garamond. Big italics are show-stoppers. Cyrillic present. Trade-off: very thin in places — fragile in body sizes — and the romanticism leans more "Vogue" than "field guide." Use as display-only, pair with a sturdier body.

№ 05

Source Serif 4

The safe-modern pick. Adobe Originals, variable with optical-size axis, excellent Cyrillic. Calm, contemporary, very legible. Trade-off: less italic drama and less "publication" character than the others — closer to a refined product UI than a literary reference.
